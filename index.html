How Brand Rate works

Brand Rate uses a smooth audience-size pricing anchor with broad benchmark guardrails, then adjusts for reach performance, engagement, primary audience market, niche and deliverable complexity to create a practical starting range for sponsorship negotiations.

Why follower count is not enough

Two creators with the same follower count can deliver very different reach. That is why the calculator gives average views or reach meaningful weight and adjusts for engagement.

Why Brand Rate avoids hard creator tiers

Creator pricing does not suddenly change because an account gains one follower and crosses an arbitrary tier. Brand Rate uses smooth interpolation between broad audience-size benchmark points, then lets reach and engagement move the estimate within guardrails.

Why audience location matters

Brand campaign budgets and the commercial value of audience reach vary across markets. Brand Rate uses broad market adjustments as an estimation input and shows estimates in the pricing currency associated with the selected audience market. Mixed/global and other-market estimates use USD as a neutral international display currency. These are benchmark-calibrated pricing outputs, not live foreign-exchange conversions, and cannot represent every brand, niche or campaign.
